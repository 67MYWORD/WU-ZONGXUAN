## 1. Planning & Research (10%)

### 1.1 Concept / Treatment
**City:** Xi'an, Shaanxi Province, China  
**Target Audience:** Culturally curious travellers aged 25-40 (Millennials & Gen Z) who seek authentic, off-the-beaten-path experiences rather than mass tourism.  
**Unique Angle:** "Where Ancient Walls Meet Modern Flavors."  
**Core Message:** Xi'an is not a city trapped in history books; it is a living, breathing organism where 3,100 years of imperial legacy coexists with a vibrant, messy, and delicious street food culture.

### 1.2 Case Study & Inspiration
I analyzed three tourism websites to inform my UX strategy:
| Website | Strength Adopted | Weakness Avoided |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Visit Copenhagen** | Clean white space, high-quality hero video loop, intuitive card navigation. | Over-reliance on video slowed load time. I opted for optimized static images. |
| **Eat Tokyo (Local Guide)** | Deep categorization of food niches (Ramen vs. Sushi deep dives). | Too much text on mobile. I used a grid layout with "Pro Tip" callout boxes. |
| **National Parks (US)** | Strong visual hierarchy and "Plan Your Visit" footer utility. | Corporate tone. I used warmer, more personal language. |

### 1.3 Influence
The design is influenced by the **Tang Dynasty color palette** (Red #B33B2C for walls/authority, Gold #E6B422 for prosperity) and **Brutalist web design** trends (large typography, distinct card borders) to give a modern edge to an ancient subject.

## 9. Reflective Report: Process & Perception (10%)

### 9.1 Process
The project began with a broad concept of "show Xi'an" which I narrowed to the specific angle of "food and history intersection." The biggest technical challenge was optimizing images for Web without losing the rich texture of the street photography. I used **Squoosh** to compress images to under 150KB while maintaining visual quality.

The decision to create **four distinct pages** (Home, Food, Heritage, Festivals) rather than a single scrolling page was deliberate. It allows for deeper SEO targeting and makes the content less overwhelming for the user.

### 9.2 Perception (Looking Forward)
If this were a real client project, I would:
1.  **Integrate a Map API:** Allow users to save food stall locations directly to Google Maps from the site.
2.  **Add Audio:** Include ambient sound buttons (e.g., "Listen to the Bell Tower chime" or "Sounds of the Night Market").
3.  **A/B Test:** Test two different hero images (Night Bell Tower vs. Daytime Terracotta) to see which drives more clicks to the food page.
